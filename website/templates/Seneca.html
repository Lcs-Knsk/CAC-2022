Seneca State Forest has 11,68 acres of forest bordering the Greenbrier River in Pocahontas County. Established in 1924, it is the oldest of WV's State Forests.

Seneca has cabins, a lake for trout, more fishing, hunting, campgrounds, and lots of mountains. People come to Seneca to hike the 23 miles of forest trails, and others come to bike the 40 miles worth of trails and roads.
